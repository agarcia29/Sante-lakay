How It Works

From sign-up to dispatch — one connected system.

Enrolling your family in Haiti takes minutes. Behind that simplicity is a coordinated network that's ready the moment someone needs it.

Getting Enrolled

Four steps, from wherever you are.

01

Choose a membership plan

Select Individual or Family/Group coverage based on how many people in Haiti you want to protect. Add extra members to a Family plan at any time.

2 minutes
02

Add your family members in Haiti

Enter each person's name and local Haiti phone number. You can also let us know which region of Haiti they live in, which helps us prioritize where to expand our Guardian Network.

Names & phone numbers
03

Your family gets notified — in Kreyòl

Each family member receives a text message in Haitian Creole letting them know they're now covered, with a short explanation of the service and the dispatch number to call in an emergency.

Automatic SMS
04

Set up recurring billing

Your card is charged monthly or annually. Receipts are sent automatically, and if a payment fails we'll notify you before any coverage lapse.

Monthly or annual
In An Emergency

What happens when your family calls.

One phone number. A trained team. A coordinated response — every time.

STEP 1

Your family calls the Sante Lakay dispatch number, available 24/7 from our call center in Port-au-Prince.

STEP 2

The dispatcher verifies membership status using the member's name and birthdate — no cards or codes needed.

STEP 3

The team coordinates the right response: ground ambulance, a Guardian Network doctor, or air evacuation.

STEP 4

A patient advocate stays involved, communicating with hospitals and keeping the family abroad informed.

Beyond The City

Reaching families outside Port-au-Prince.

Emergency response looks different in rural Haiti. That's why we built the Guardian Network — local doctors and medical professionals stationed in areas HERO doesn't cover full-time.

  • Basic life-saving equipment on hand at all times
  • Affiliated with local hospitals and clinics
  • Transport to the nearest airfield for evacuation when needed
While You're Abroad
